# Case: SaaS MSA Contract Review

## Prompt

Use `/legal-contract-review` to review this SaaS MSA excerpt for a customer-side reviewer under New York law. The customer is a healthcare technology company and wants a practical risk list plus fallback language.

## Excerpt

Vendor may suspend the Services at any time if Vendor believes Customer's use may create risk to Vendor or any third party. Customer is responsible for all use of the Services. Vendor disclaims all warranties. Vendor's total liability will not exceed the fees paid in the one month before the claim. Customer will indemnify Vendor from all claims arising out of Customer data, use of the Services, or breach of this Agreement. Vendor may use Customer data to improve its products. Either party may terminate for convenience on 90 days' notice. Sections intended to survive will survive.

## Expected Qualities

- Flags unilateral suspension discretion and requests objective triggers, notice, and cure where possible.
- Flags healthcare/data sensitivity and limits product-improvement use of customer data.
- Challenges one-month liability cap and broad warranty disclaimer.
- Narrows customer indemnity and adds vendor indemnity for IP, security, confidentiality, and law violations.
- Fixes vague survival language.
- Provides redline-ready fallback language.
