# LegalCode Brand Guide

LegalCode is a local-first legal AI workspace for litigation matters. The brand should feel calm, precise, private, and dependable. It should never sound like it is replacing lawyers or automating legal judgment without review.

## One-Line Description

LegalCode is a local-first AI workspace for litigators that keeps drafting, research, evidence, deadlines, and collaboration tied to verified matter sources and human approval.

## Short Description

LegalCode helps solo litigators and small legal teams organize matters, draft legal work, verify citations and facts, manage evidence and deadlines, and collaborate on legal documents and structured work tables. It is built for supervised automation: AI can draft, classify, extract, suggest, and check, but final legal work remains human-approved.

## Positioning Statement

For solo litigators and small legal teams, LegalCode is a local-first legal AI workspace that combines matter management, legal drafting, structured work tables, source verification, and supervised AI coworkers. Unlike generic chatbots, LegalCode keeps work matter-scoped, source-linked, auditable, and human-approved.

## Voice

LegalCode should sound:

- precise,
- calm,
- practical,
- evidence-driven,
- confidentiality-aware,
- respectful of lawyer judgment.

Avoid:

- hype about replacing lawyers,
- guarantees of outcomes,
- "fully automated filing" language,
- vague AI magic,
- broad claims that cannot be tied to source verification.

## Product Principles

- **Matter first:** legal work belongs inside a client/matter context.
- **Sources before certainty:** facts, quotes, dates, and citations need source spans or unresolved-question labels.
- **Human approval before final:** AI output remains draft/suggestion/comment/extraction/final candidate until approved.
- **Private by default:** local-first storage, invite-based collaboration, no public link sharing.
- **Bring your own keys/accounts:** OSS integrations do not bundle paid legal data or shared vendor credentials.
- **Memory is continuity, not authority:** optional memory can help recall prior decisions, but verified law and facts must come from sources.

## Naming

Use **LegalCode** as the product name.

Use **LegalCode Desktop** for the standalone app.

Use **LegalCode Matter Command Center** for the primary matter workspace.

Use **LegalCode Coworkers** for role-specific agents:

- Case Strategist,
- Research Clerk,
- Drafting Clerk,
- Discovery Analyst,
- Citation Checker,
- Deadline Clerk,
- Evidence Chronologist,
- Settlement Analyst,
- Filing Assistant,
- Workspace Integrator.

Avoid names that imply autonomous law practice, such as "lawyer bot", "auto attorney", or "AI lawyer".

## Visual Direction

LegalCode should look like professional legal infrastructure, not a consumer chatbot.

Recommended feel:

- restrained,
- document-forward,
- high contrast,
- dense but readable,
- clear status markers,
- subtle color,
- audit and verification states visible.

Suggested palette direction:

- ink: deep charcoal,
- paper: warm white,
- rule: cool gray,
- trust: blue,
- attention: amber,
- verified: green,
- blocked/rejected: red.

Use color as status, not decoration.

## UI Language

Prefer:

- "Draft"
- "Suggestion"
- "Source-linked"
- "Unverified"
- "Needs review"
- "Human approved"
- "Ready to export"
- "Blocked: citation unresolved"

Avoid:

- "Guaranteed"
- "Court-ready" without approval
- "Autofile"
- "Verified" without source resolution
- "Done" for unapproved legal work

## Disclaimer

LegalCode is legal workflow software and AI-assisted drafting/research infrastructure. It does not create an attorney-client relationship, provide legal advice by itself, or replace review by qualified counsel.

LegalCode is built on the OpenCode runtime but is not built by, endorsed by, or affiliated with the OpenCode team.
