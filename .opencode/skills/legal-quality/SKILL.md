# Legal Quality

Use this skill whenever the user asks for legal analysis, drafting, research, contract review, compliance mapping, legal operations process design, or matter organization.

## Intake Checklist

Before finalizing legal work, account for:

- Jurisdiction, governing law, forum, and relevant dates.
- User role, counterparty role, procedural posture, and business objective.
- Source posture: provided documents, cited authorities, web sources, internal policies, or no sources.
- Output type: memo, issue list, redline comments, clause language, checklist, table, chronology, or action plan.

Ask only for missing information that materially changes the work. If the user wants momentum, proceed with clearly stated assumptions.

## Output Standard

- State assumptions and scope briefly.
- Separate facts, issues, analysis, and recommendations.
- Label confidence and unresolved questions.
- Use risk levels only when they help the user prioritize.
- Provide exact proposed language for drafting tasks.
- Include citations or source references when source-backed legal claims are requested.
- Avoid filler disclaimers. Use a short counsel-review note only when the matter is jurisdiction-specific, high-stakes, or source-limited.
- Mark litigation deadlines as proposed until the triggering event, forum, rule/order, service method, and lawyer approval are present.
- Mark filing packages as not final until a human approval gate is satisfied.
- Treat citations, quotes, party names, dates, and exhibit references as unverified unless tied to a provided or retrieved source span.

## Review Heuristics

For contracts, check:

- Parties, authority, dates, signatures, and defined terms.
- Scope, deliverables, acceptance, pricing, invoicing, taxes, expenses, and changes.
- Confidentiality, data protection, IP ownership, licenses, publicity, and records.
- Warranties, covenants, compliance with laws, audit, security, and insurance.
- Indemnity, liability caps, excluded damages, remedies, termination, survival, and transition.
- Assignment, subcontracting, notices, dispute resolution, governing law, venue, force majeure, and order of precedence.

For compliance, check:

- Requirement, source, applicability, control, evidence, owner, cadence, gap, severity, and remediation.

For research, check:

- Source hierarchy, jurisdiction, recency, negative treatment or supersession risk, procedural posture, and open questions.

For solo-litigator matters, check:

- Forum, procedural posture, claims, defenses, deadlines, service method, evidence sources, witness list, damages, settlement posture, and filing-readiness.
- Agent provenance: matter context used, artifacts read, source spans relied on, output kind, verification status, confidence, unresolved questions, and human approval status.
